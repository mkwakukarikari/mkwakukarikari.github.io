About

I am a doctoral candidate in Geography with a specialization in Urban and Regional Planning at the University of Alabama. My research sits at the intersection of urban planning, transportation, and health geography, with particular attention to underserved regions in the United States and West Africa.

My current work examines diabetes prevalence and healthcare accessibility in Alabama's Black Belt region, applying geospatial and statistical methods to surface patterns of health disparity. In parallel, I study active and sustainable mobility, including bike-sharing, motorcycle taxis, and non-motorized transport, and how urban form and policy shape access in African cities.

I serve as a Graduate Research Assistant in the Department of Geography and the Environment at the University of Alabama. I previously completed a Transportation Planning Internship at Toole Design Group in Atlanta.
