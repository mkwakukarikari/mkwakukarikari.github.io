Network & Accessibility Analysis

Applied network-based spatial analysis to model movement, accessibility, and connectivity across urban and regional systems. Techniques include shortest path routing, origin–destination (OD) matrix development and flow mapping, accessibility analysis, and network connectivity measures (e.g., Shimbel index) to support transportation and spatial planning decisions.

Mapped and analyzed proposed BRT corridors in Accra and Kumasi to assess connectivity, corridor coverage, and alignment with major activity centers. The maps highlight priority routes and their potential to improve urban mobility and reduce congestion.

Traffic Analysis Zone (TAZ) Income Assessment for BRT Planning

Analyzed income distribution across Traffic Analysis Zones (TAZs) to evaluate equity and accessibility implications of proposed BRT corridors. The maps identify underserved low-income areas and inform route prioritization for more inclusive transit planning.
