Atlanta Regional Commission TDM Innovation Study & Pilots

2025 – 2026

Atlanta, Georgia

Project Overview

This project supports the Atlanta Regional Commission in advancing its Transportation Demand Management (TDM) strategy through a series of innovative pilot programs. The initiative focuses on improving access to sustainable transportation options particularly for underserved and historically under-engaged communities.

Working as part of a team at Toole Design Group, I contributed to the development and implementation of three targeted micropilots designed to test new approaches to outreach, behavior change, and multimodal commuting.

Problem Statement

Traditional TDM programs often struggle to effectively reach:

  • Low-income communities
  • Individuals with disabilities
  • Suburban and rural populations
  • Communities with limited transit access

This project aimed to design and test community-centered and behavior-informed strategies to improve participation in sustainable transportation programs.

My Role

  • Led and supported community engagement initiatives to promote multimodal transportation and increase adoption of active mobility options
  • Provided one-on-one travel behavior support, including personalized route planning and guided practice trips
  • Delivered hands-on outreach and education through workshops, community events, and transportation training sessions
  • Facilitated MyGCO platform training and demonstrations to improve user engagement with regional TDM tools

Accra Intelligent Traffic Management System

2021 – 2023

Accra, Ghana

Project Overview

This project involved the implementation of an Intelligent Traffic Management System (ITMS) in Accra to improve urban mobility through real-time traffic monitoring and signal optimization. Led by the Department of Urban Roads, the initiative focused on enhancing the efficiency of major intersections and reducing congestion across key corridors in the Greater Accra Metropolitan Area.

Problem Statement

Accra experiences severe and persistent traffic congestion due to:

  • Inefficient and manually operated traffic signal systems
  • Rapid urban growth and increasing vehicle demand
  • Limited real-time traffic monitoring and response capabilities
  • Poor coordination across major intersections

These challenges result in increased travel time, fuel consumption, and reduced overall network efficiency.

My Role

  • Supported traffic data collection and intersection analysis
  • Assisted with monitoring traffic signal operations along key corridors
  • Contributed to project coordination and implementation activities
  • Supported planning efforts related to traffic flow improvement and congestion reduction

Public Transport Sector Enhancement (BRT Study)

2020

Kumasi, Ghana

Project Overview

This project focused on improving public transportation in Kumasi through the development of a Bus Rapid Transit (BRT) system. The initiative aimed to address increasing travel demand and enhance the efficiency and reliability of urban transport services. This project was conducted in collaboration with ROM Transportation Engineering Limited.

Problem Statement

Rapid urban growth in Kumasi has led to increased travel demand and congestion, while existing transport systems face several challenges:

  • Limited capacity of current public transport systems
  • Heavy reliance on informal paratransit services
  • Inefficient routing and operational structures
  • Lack of data-driven planning for future mobility needs

This project aimed to develop a structured, high-capacity transport system supported by data, planning, and institutional coordination.

My Role

  • Supported the feasibility study for a Bus Rapid Transit (BRT) system
  • Conducted traffic counts and mobility data collection across key urban corridors
  • Produced transport network maps and route analyses to support planning decisions
  • Assisted with public transport system assessment and planning
  • Participated in stakeholder engagement and coordination activities

Municipality Action Plan – Victoria, Laguna

2022

Victoria, Philippines

Project Overview

This project involved the development of an integrated action plan for the Municipality of Victoria, Laguna, aimed at promoting sustainable urban development across social, economic, and environmental dimensions. The work was conducted as part of an international, multidisciplinary planning team under the IHS Sustainable Urban Development program.

The project combined policy analysis, stakeholder engagement, and strategic planning to address key development challenges in a rapidly growing, agriculture-based municipality.

Problem Statement

Victoria faces interconnected development challenges, including:

  • Poor household waste management and low participation in waste segregation
  • Limited economic opportunities, particularly for youth and returning workers
  • Environmental degradation, especially water pollution in Laguna Lake

These issues contribute to unsustainable urban growth and declining environmental and economic resilience.

My Role

  • Contributed to the Social Planning Team, focusing on community behavior and waste management strategies
  • Conducted problem analysis and stakeholder mapping to identify barriers to effective waste segregation
  • Supported the design of a community-based incentive system to improve household waste practices
  • Participated in participatory planning sessions and strategy development workshops
  • Contributed to the development of implementation timelines, monitoring frameworks, and action plans
